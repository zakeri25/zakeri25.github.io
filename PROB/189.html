Viewing determinant as a smooth function $\det: \M_n(\RR) \cong \RR^{n^2} \to \RR$, find a formula for the derivative $D\det$.

First find the linear map $D\det(I)$, which is a well-known invariant.

It is clear that for every $B \in \M_n(\RR)$, $$ \det(I+\ve B)=1+\ve \tr(B) + O(\ve^2) \qquad \text{as} \ \ve \to 0. $$ This shows $D\det(I)$ is the linear map $B \mapsto \tr(B)$. To find the derivative of $\det$ at an arbitrary point $A \in \M_n(\RR)$, first suppose $A$ is invertible and consider the linear isomorphism $\Phi: \M_n(\RR) \to \M_n(\RR)$ defined by $\Phi(X)=A^{-1}X$ which sends $A$ to $I$ and satisfies $$ \det \circ \, \Phi = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \ \det. $$ By the chain rule, $$ D\det(I) \circ D\Phi(A) = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \ D\det(A) $$ so $$ D\det(A)(B) = \det(A) \tr(A^{-1}B). $$ In other words, $$ D\det(A): B \mapsto \tr \big(\operatorname{adj}(A)B \big). $$ Since invertible matrices are dense in $\M_n(\RR)$ and $\det$ is a smooth function, this formula should hold for all $A \in \M_n(\RR)$.

Remark. As a special case, it follows that for any smooth path $t \mapsto A(t)$ in $\M_n(\RR)$, $$ (\det(A(t)))' = D\det(A(t))(A'(t)) = \tr\big(\operatorname{adj}(A(t))A'(t) \big). $$ This formula can be used to give a quick proof (among many others!) for the formula $\det(e^A)=e^{\tr(A)}$. Setting $f(t)=\det(e^{tA})$, we obtain the differential equation $$ f'(t)=\tr\big(\operatorname{adj}(e^{tA})Ae^{tA} \big)=f(t)\tr\big(e^{-tA}Ae^{tA} \big)=f(t) \tr(A). $$ with the initial condition $f(0)=1$, which has the unique solution $f(t)=e^{t \tr(A)}$.
