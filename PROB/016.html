Show that every maximal compact subgroup $K$ of $\GL_n(\RR)$ is conjugate to the orthogonal group $O(n)$.

First construct a $K$-invariant inner product on $\RR^n$ by a standard averaging process.

Let $\langle \cdot , \cdot \rangle$ denote the Euclidean inner product on $\RR^n$. For $x,y\in \RR^n$, define $$ \sigma(x,y)= \int_K \langle gx,gy \rangle \ d\mu(g), $$ where $\mu$ is the left-invariant Haar measure on $K$. It is easy to see that $\sigma$ is a $K$-invariant inner product on $\RR^n$. Take an element $g\in \GL_n(\RR)$ such that $\sigma(x,y)=\langle gx, gy \rangle$. Then $gKg^{-1}\subset O(n)$ since for every $k \in K$, $$ \langle gkg^{-1}x,gkg^{-1}y \rangle=\sigma(kg^{-1}x,kg^{-1}y)=\sigma(g^{-1}x,g^{-1}y)=\langle x,y \rangle. $$ Now $gKg^{-1}=O(n)$ by maximality of $K$.
