Prove that every periodic orientation-preserving diffeomorphism $f: \Sen^2 \to \Sen^2$ is smoothly conjugate to an element of $\SO(3)$.

Construct an $f$-invariant conformal structure on the sphere by averaging.

Let $p>1$ be the order of $f$. Let $\mu_0$ be the standard conformal structure of $\Sen^2$ represented by the zero Beltrami differential and define a new conformal structure $\mu = \mu(z) d\ov{z}/dz$, where for each $z \in \DD$ the coefficient $\mu(z)$ is the "conformal barycenter" of the $p$ points $((f^{\circ j})^{\ast} \mu_0) (z)$ for $0 \leq j \leq p-1$ in the unit disk. Since the pull-back operator $f^{\ast}$ acts as a Möbius automorphism on Beltrami coefficients, $f^{\ast}\mu =\mu$ by conformal naturality. By the measurable Riemann mapping theorem there is a diffeomorphism $h: \Sen^2 \to \Sen^2$ such that $h^{\ast}\mu_0 =\mu$. The diffeomorphism $g:\Sen^2 \to \Sen^2$ defined by $g=h \circ f \circ h^{-1}$ preserves $\mu_0$, hence it is conformal. As a periodic element of $\PSL_2(\CC)$, $g$ has to be an element of $\SO(3)$.

Remark. The statement of the problem is true under the much weaker assumption that $f$ is merely a homeomorphism. This is a theorem of Kerékjártó; see A. Constantin and B. Kolev, The theorem of Kerékjártó on periodic homeomorphisms of the disk and the sphere, L’Enseignement Mathématique, 40 (1994) 193-204. The above proof works for any periodic quasiconformal homeomorphism. It can be generalized to show that any uniformly quasiconformal group of homeomorphisms of a Riemann surface is quasiconformally conjugate to a group of conformal automorphisms. This is first observed by D. Sullivan. See S. Zakeri's online note On a theorem of Kerékjártó at https://qcpages.qc.cuny.edu/~zakeri/notes/ker.pdf.
