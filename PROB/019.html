Let $f,g:X \to X$ be conformal automorphisms of a compact hyperbolic Riemann surface $X$. If $f$ is homotopic to $g$, show that $f=g$.

Think of how $f,g$ act on closed geodesics of $X$. Lift to the universal covering.

It suffices to consider the case where $g=\id_X$. Then $f_{\ast}$ acts on $\pi_1(X)$ as the identity map. Since every homotopy class in $\pi_1(X)$ is represented by a unique closed geodesic, we see that $f(\alpha)=\alpha$ for any closed geodesic $\alpha$ in $X$. If $F: \DD \to \DD$ is a lift of $f$ to the universal covering, it follows that $F(\tilde \alpha )=\tilde \alpha$, where $\tilde \alpha$ is the lift of $\alpha$. Thus, $F$ fixes the two endpoints of $\tilde \alpha$ on the unit circle. Since $X$ is hyperbolic, it has at least two closed geodesics that are non-homotopic, and therefore their lifts to $\DD$ do not have the same endpoints. It follows that $F$ fixes at least $3$ points on the unit circle. Hence $F=\id_{\DD}$.
