Let $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_k$ be permutations in the symmetric group $S_n$ with $\sigma_1 \cdots \sigma_k=1$. Suppose the subgroup of $S_n$ generated by $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_k$ acts transitively on $\{ 1, \ldots, n \}$. Show that $$ \nu(\sigma_1)+\cdots+\nu(\sigma_k) \leq (k-2)n+2, $$ where $\nu(\sigma_i)$ stands for the number of cycles in the canonical decomposition of $\sigma_i$.

Think of the classical construction of compact Riemann surfaces as branched coverings of the sphere, and apply the Riemann-Hurwitz formula.

This proof is due to the late Korean-Canadian mathematician Rimhak Ree. Consider the Riemann sphere with distinct marked points $b, p_1, \ldots, p_k$ and loops $\gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_k$ starting and ending at $b$, such that each $\gamma_i$ winds once around $p_i$ counterclockwise and does not wind around other $p_j$. Construct an $n$-sheeted branched covering $f: X \to \Chat$, ramified over $\{ p_1, \ldots, p_k \}$, for which the action of $\gamma_i$ on the fiber $f^{-1}(b)$ realizes $\sigma_i$. Here $X$ is a compact orientable surface and the transitivity assumption guarantees that $X$ is connected. Moreover, the number of critical points in $f^{-1}(p_i)$, counting multiplicities, is precisely $n-\nu(\sigma_i)$. In fact, if $\sigma_i$ is the product of $\nu=\nu(\sigma_i)$ disjoint cycles $\tau_1, \ldots, \tau_\nu$ of lengths $\ell_1, \ldots, \ell_\nu$, each cycle $\tau_j$ identifies $\ell_j$ of the preimages of $p_i$, hence contributes $\ell_j-1$ critical points counting multiplicities. This gives a total number of $$ (\ell_1-1)+\cdots+(\ell_\nu-1)=n-\nu $$ critical points in the fiber $f^{-1}(p_i)$. Now, by the Riemann-Hurwitz formula, $$ \sum_{i=1}^k (n-\nu(\sigma_i)) = n \chi(\Chat) - \chi(X) = 2n -\chi(X) \geq 2n-2, $$ which yields the desired inequality.
