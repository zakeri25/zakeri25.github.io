Let $f: [0,1] \to [0,1]$ be continuous and $\{ x_n:=f^{\circ n}(x_0) \}_{n \in \NN}$ be an orbit of $f$ such that $\lim_{n \to \infty} (x_{n+1}-x_n)=0$. Show that $\{ x_n \}$ converges.

Assume the $x_n$ are all distinct and let $K$ be the set of all accumulation points of $\{ x_n \}$. Check that the existence of two distinct points in $K$ would lead to a contradiction.

We may suppose that the $x_n$ are all distinct. The assumption $\lim_{n \to \infty} (x_{n+1}-x_n)=0$ implies that every accumulation point of $\{ x_n \}$ is a fixed point of $f$. Let $K$ be the set of all accumulation points of $\{ x_n \}$. Then $K$ is non-empty, compact, and without interior; in particular, $K \neq [0,1]$. Suppose $K$ contains two points $0 \leq p < q \leq 1$. Let $(a,b)$ be a connected component of $[p,q] \sm K$ and set $\ve:=(b-a)/3 > 0$. Choose $N \geq 1$ such that the $\ve$-jump property $|x_{n+1}-x_n| < \ve$ holds for all $n \geq N$. Find integer sequences $N < m_1 < n_1 < m_2 < n_2 < \cdots$ such that $|x_{m_i}-a| < \ve$ and $|x_{n_i}-b| < \ve$ for all $i$. It follows easily from the $\ve$-jump property that there are integers $m_i < k_i < n_i$ for which $x_{k_i} \in (a+\ve,b-\ve)$ for all $i$. But then any accumulation point of $\{ x_{k_i} \}$ would be in $[a+\ve,b-\ve] \cap K$, a contradiction.
