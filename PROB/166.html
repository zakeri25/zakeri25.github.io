Let $\omega=\exp(2\pi i/n)$ for some integer $n \geq 2$. Show that $\ds{\sum_{j=1}^{n-1} |1-\omega^j|^{-2}=\frac{n^2-1}{12}}$.

Prove the equivalent identity $\sum_{j=1}^{n-1} \cot^2 (\pi j/n) = (n-1)(n-2)/3$. The binomial expansion of $\exp(int)=(\cos t + i \sin t)^n$ may be helpful.

We may assume $n \geq 3$ since the result trivially holds for $n=2$. The computation \begin{align*} |1-\omega^j|^2 & =(1-\omega^j)(1-\ov{\omega}^j)=2-2\myre(\omega^j) \\ & =4 \sin^2 \Big( \frac{\pi j}{n} \Big) \end{align*} together with $\csc^2 x = 1+ \cot^2 x$ shows that the claim is equivalent to the identity $$ \sum_{j=1}^{n-1} \cot^2 \Big( \frac{\pi j}{n} \Big) = \frac{1}{3} (n-1)(n-2). $$ To see the latter, take the imaginary part of the binomial expansion $$ \exp(i n t) = (\cos t + i \sin t)^n = \sum_{k=0}^n {n \choose k} \ i^k \ \cos^{n-k} t \ \sin^k t $$ to obtain $$ \sin (nt) = \sum_{k=1}^{\lceil n/2 \rceil} {n \choose 2k-1} \ (-1)^{k-1} \ \cos^{n-2k+1} t \ \sin^{2k-1} t $$ or $$ \frac{\sin (nt)}{\sin^n t} = \sum_{k=1}^{\lceil n/2 \rceil} {n \choose 2k-1} \ (-1)^{k-1} \ \cot^{n-2k+1} t. $$ Since the left hand side vanishes at $t=\pi j/n$ for $1 \leq j \leq n-1$, it follows that $x_j:=\cot(\pi j/n)$ are the $n-1$ roots of the polynomial \[ \sum_{k=1}^{\lceil n/2 \rceil} {n \choose 2k-1} \ (-1)^{k-1} \ x^{n-2k+1} = {n \choose 1} x^{n-1} - {n \choose 3} x^{n-3} + \cdots. \] Hence, \begin{align*} \sum_{j=1}^{n-1} x_j^2 & = \Big( \sum_{j=1}^{n-1} x_j \Big)^2 - 2 \sum_{1 \leq j < k \leq n-1} x_j x_k = - 2 \sum_{1 \leq j < k \leq n-1} x_j x_k \\ & = 2 \frac{{n \choose 3}}{{n \choose 1}} = \frac{1}{3} (n-1)(n-2). \end{align*}
