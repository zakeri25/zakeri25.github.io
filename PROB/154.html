Suppose $f$ and $g$ are holomorphic functions in a domain $U$ and there is an $\alpha>0$ such that $|f|^\alpha+|g|^\alpha=1$ in $U$. Show that $f$ and $g$ must be constant.

Fix $p \in U$ where $f(p),g(p) \neq 0$ and apply the maximum principle to the function $h:= (\lambda f)^\alpha + (\mu g)^\alpha$ in a neighborhood of $p$ for suitable unimodular complex numbers $\lambda,\mu$.

If $f$ vanishes somewhere in $U$, then $|g|$ reaches a maximum value of $1$ at that point, so $g$ and therefore $f$ must be constant. So without loss of generality we may assume $f,g$ are nowhere vanishing. Take any $p \in U$ and find unimodular complex numbers $\lambda,\mu$ such that $\lambda f(p)=|f(p)|$ and $\mu g(p)=|g(p)|$. Choose holomorphic branches of the function $w \mapsto w^\alpha$ in small neighborhoods of $|f(p)|$ and $|g(p)|$ and define $h:=(\lambda f)^\alpha + (\mu g)^\alpha$, which is then holomorphic in a small disk $\DD(p,r) \subset U$. We have $|h| \leq |f|^\alpha+|g|^\alpha=1$ and $h(p)=1$, so by the maximum principle $h=1$ throughout $\DD(p,r)$. Thus, $$ |(\lambda f)^\alpha|+|1-(\lambda f)^\alpha| = |\lambda f|^\alpha + |\mu g|^\alpha =1 $$ which means $(\lambda f)^\alpha$ belongs to the interval $[0,1]$. This shows $(\lambda f)^\alpha$ restricted to $\DD(p,r)$ is not an open map, hence it is constant. Thus $f$ and therefore $g$ must be constant in $\DD(p,r)$. The result follows since $U$ is connected.
