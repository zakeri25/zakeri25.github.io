Suppose $a_1 \geq a_2 \geq a_3 \geq \cdots > 0$ and $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = +\infty$. If $A \subset \NN$ is a set of positive lower density in the sense that $\liminf_{n \to \infty} |A \cap [1,n]|/n >0$, show that $\sum_{n \in A} a_n = +\infty$.

Look at the partition of $A$ into the sets $A \cap [n_{k-1},n_k)$, where $n_k=2^{kp}$ for a fixed sufficiently large integer $p$.

Take an integer $p$ such that $|A \cap [1,n]| \geq 2^{-p+1} n$ for all large $n$. Set $n_k:= 2^{kp}$. Then, for all $k \geq$ some $k_0$, \begin{align*} |A \cap [n_{k-1},n_k)| & = |A \cap [1,n_k)| - |A \cap [1,n_{k-1})| \\ & \geq 2^{-p+1} n_k - n_{k-1} = 2^{-p} n_k. \end{align*} Thus, \begin{align*} \sum_{n \in A}^{\infty} a_n & \geq \sum_{k = k_0}^{\infty} \sum_{n \in A \cap [n_{k-1},n_k)} a_n \geq 2^{-p} \sum_{k= k_0}^{\infty} n_k a_{n_k} \\ & = 2^{-2p} \sum_{k= k_0}^{\infty} n_{k+1} a_{n_k} \geq 2^{-2p} \sum_{k= k_0}^{\infty} \sum_{n \in [n_k,n_{k+1})} a_n \\ & = 2^{-2p} \sum_{n=n_{k_0}}^{\infty} a_n =+\infty. \end{align*} Remark. The lower density in the hypothesis cannot be replaced with the upper density. For example, let $A$ be the union of the integer segments $[2^{2^k},2^{2^k+1})$ for $k \geq 1$. It is clear that $A$ has upper density $\geq 1/2$. But if $a_n=1/(n \log n)$, then \begin{align*} \sum_{n \in A}^{\infty} a_n & = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \sum_{n \in [2^{2^k},2^{2^k+1})} \frac{1}{n \log n} \\ & \leq \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} 2^{2^k} \cdot \frac{1}{2^{2^k} \ 2^k \log 2} = \frac{1}{\log 2} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} 2^{-k} <+\infty. \end{align*}
