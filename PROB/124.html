Show that two distinct ellipses with a common focus cannot meet in more than two points.

*** INCOMPLETE PROOF ***

Consider the first ellipse $E$ with foci $p,q$ and the second ellipse $E_e$ with foci $p,q'$ and eccentricity $e$. We fix the foci $p,q'$ of $E_e$ but vary $e$. Evidently $|E \cap E_e|=0$ as $e \to 0$. Suppose $|E \cap E_e|>2$ for some $e$. After a small perturbation we may assume $|E \cap E_e|=4$ and all intersections are transversal. It follows by continuity that there are values $0 < e_2 \leq e_1 < e$ such that $E, E_{e_1}$ are tangent at some $x_1$ and $E, E_{e_2}$ are tangent at some $x_2$, with $x_1 \neq x_2$. Now the lines $x_1p$ and $x_1q$ and $x_1q'$ make equal angles to the common tangent line to $E, E_{e_1}$ at $x_1$, so $q'$ lies on the line $x_1q$. Similarly, $q'$ lies on $x_2q$. This implies $x_1,x_2,q,q'$, all on the same line.
