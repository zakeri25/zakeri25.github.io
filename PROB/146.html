Compute the fundamental group of the manifold $M = \big\{ (z,w,\zeta) \in \CC^3 \sm \{ (0,0,0) \} : \zeta^2=zw \big\}$.

Consider the map $p: \CC^2 \sm \{ (0,0) \} \to M$ defined by $p(u,v):=(u^2,v^2,uv)$

Define the holomorphic map $f: \CC^3 \sm \{ (0,0,0) \} \to \CC$ by $f(z,w,\zeta):=zw-\zeta^2$. The gradient $\nabla f(z,w,\zeta)=(w,z,-2\zeta)$ is non-zero for $(z,w,\zeta) \neq (0,0,0)$, hence $M=f^{-1}(0)$ is a complex $2$-dimensional submanifold of $\CC^3 \sm \{ (0,0,0) \}$. Consider the holomorphic map $p: \CC^2 \sm \{ (0,0) \} \to M$ defined by $p(u,v):=(u^2,v^2,uv)$ which is clearly surjective and proper. The derivative $$ Dp(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} 2u & 0 \\ 0 & 2v \\ v & u \end{bmatrix} $$ has rank $2$ everywhere, so $p$ is a local biholomorphism. It follows that $p$ is a covering map of degree $2$. Since $\CC^2 \sm \{ (0,0) \}$ has the homotopy type of $\SS^3$, it is simply connected. Hence $p$ is a universal covering map with the deck group generated by the involution $(u,v) \mapsto (-u,-v)$. We conclude that $\pi_1(M) \cong \ZZ_2$. As a generator for $\pi_1(M)$, one may choose the homotopy class of the loop $\gamma: [0,2\pi] \to M$ given by $\gamma(t)=(e^{it}, e^{it}, e^{it})$, which is non-trivial because it lifts under $p$ to the non-loop $\tilde{\gamma}(t)=(e^{it/2}, e^{it/2})$.
