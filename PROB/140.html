Suppose $G$ is a group and $G=HK$, where $H$ is an infinite cyclic and $K$ is a finite subgroup of $G$. If the normalizer $N(K)$ is strictly larger than $K$, show that $G/Z$ is a finite group, where $Z$ denotes the center of $G$.

Show that $[H: H \cap Z]$ is finite by finding a non-identity $h \in H$ with $h^n \in Z$ for some $n>0$.

Every $g \in G$ can be written as $hk$ where $h \in H$ and $k \in K$, and this representation is unique since clearly $H \cap K = \{ e \}$. Take $g=hk \in N(K)\sm K$. Then $h \in N(K) \sm K$ so $x \mapsto hxh^{-1}$ is an automorphism of the finite group $K$ and therefore has finite order. In other words, there is an integer $n>0$ such that $h^n x h^{-n} = x$ for every $x \in K$. This shows $h^n$ commutes with everything in $K$, and therefore with everything in $G$, that is, $h^n \in H \cap Z$. In particular, $H \cap Z$ is a non-trivial subgroup of $H$, hence $[H: H \cap Z]$ is finite. Also, the map $x \mapsto Hx$ is a bijection from $K$ to the set of right cosets of $H$ in $G$, so $[G:H]=|K|$ is finite as well. The result now follows from $$ |G/Z|=[G:Z] \leq [G:H \cap Z]=[G:H]\, [H:H \cap Z]. $$
