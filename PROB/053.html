Let $f:[0,1] \to \RR$ be continuously differentiable with $\int_0^1 f(x)\, dx=0$. Prove the inequality $$ \int_0^1 f(x)^2\, dx \leq \frac{1}{2}\, \int_0^1 |f'(x)|\, dx \ \int_0^1 |f(x)|\, dx. $$

Use integration by parts.

Let $v(x):=\int_0^x f(t)\, dt$ which satisfies $v(0)=v(1)=0$. Integration by parts gives $$ \int_0^1 f(x)^2\, dx = -\int_0^1 v(x) f'(x)\, dx, $$ so the claim would follow from the triangle inequality once we show that $$ \max_{0\leq x\leq 1} |v(x)| \leq \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 |f(t)|\, dt. $$ To see the latter, consider the sets $P=\{ x\in [0,1]: f(x)>0 \}$, and $N=\{ x\in [0,1]: f(x)<0 \}$ and note that by the assumption $\int_0^1 f =0$ the equalities $\int_P f = -\int_N f = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 |f|$ hold. It follows that $$ v(x) \leq \int_{[0,x] \cap P} f(t)\, dt \leq \int_P f(t)\, dt = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 |f(t)|\, dt $$ and similarly $$ v(x) \geq \int_{[0,x] \cap N} f(t)\, dt \geq \int_N f(t)\, dt = -\frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 |f(t)|\, dt. $$
