Let $X$ be a compact hyperbolic Riemann surface and let $G$ be the deck group of the universal covering $p: \HH \rightarrow X$. Prove that every element of $G$ is a hyperbolic Möbius map.

Check that parabolic elements in $G$ would give rise to arbitrarily short homotopically non-trivial closed curves on $X$.

Evidently $G$ does not contain elliptic elements since deck transformations cannot have fixed points. Assume by way of contradiction that $G$ has a parabolic element. After a suitable conjugation by a Möbius map, we may suppose this element to be a translation of the form $z \mapsto z+a$ for some $a>0$. Pick any height $h>0$ and look at the horizontal line segment $\gamma(t)=t+ih$ for $0\leq t\leq a$. Its hyperbolic length is given by $$ \len(\gamma) = \int_0^a \frac{\gamma'(t)}{\myim \gamma(t)}\, dt = \int_0^a \frac{1}{h}\, dt = \frac{a}{h}. $$ Now the image $p(\gamma)$ is a homotopically non-trivial closed curve on $X$ with $\len(p(\gamma)) \leq \len(\gamma)$ by the Schwarz lemma, so $\len(p(\gamma)) \to 0$ as $h \to \infty$. This is a contradiction since on a compact surface there is a lower bound on the length of non-trivial closed curves.
