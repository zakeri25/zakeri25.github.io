Take an $A \in \M_n(\CC)$ with $\tr(A) \neq 0$. Show that the linear map $T: \M_n(\CC) \to \M_n(\CC)$ defined by $T(X)=\tr(A)X-\tr(X)A$ is diagonalizable.

Identify the eigenvalues of $T$ and use a dimension count.

Observe that $T(X)$ is trace-free for every $X \in \M_n(\CC)$. If $T(X)=\lambda X$, then $\tr(\lambda X)=0$, so either $\lambda=0$ or $\tr(X)=0$ in which case $T(X)=\tr(A)X$, so $\lambda=\tr(A)$. Thus, the eigenvalues of $T$ can only be $0$ or $\tr(A)$. Now $T(X)=0$ if and only if $X$ is a scalar multiple of $A$. Thus, $\lambda=0$ is an eigenvalue and its associated eigenspace has dimension $1$. Similarly, $T(X)=\tr(A) X$ if and only if $\tr(X)=0$. Thus, $\lambda=\tr(A)$ is also an eigenvalue and its associated eigenspace is precisely the kernel of $\tr: \M_n(\CC) \to \CC$, so it has dimension $n^2-1$. Since the dimensions of the eigenspaces of $T$ add up to $n^2$, we conclude that $T$ is diagonalizable.

Remark. As an amusing byproduct, we see that $\tr(T)=(n^2-1)\tr(A)$.
