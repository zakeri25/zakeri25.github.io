Show that every element of a finite field $F$ can be written as the sum of two squares.

Assuming the characteristic of $F$ is not $2$, consider the set of all squares in $F$.

Take $\alpha \in F$. Let $q$ be the order of $F$. If the characteristic of $F$ is $2$, then $q=2^r$ for some $r\geq 1$ and the result is immediate because $\alpha=(\alpha^{q/2})^2+0^2 $. So assume the characteristic of $F$ is not 2 and let $S:=\{ x^2 : x \in F \}$. Then $S$ contains $0$ and every non-zero pair of elements $\pm x$ in $F$ give rise to the same element in $S$. Therefore, $|S|=1+(q-1)/2$. Now $S\cap (\alpha -S)$ cannot be empty, because otherwise one would get $2(1+(q-1)/2)=q+1$ distinct elements in $F$.
