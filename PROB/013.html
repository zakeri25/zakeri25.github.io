Let $G$ be a Lie group, $X$ be a left-invariant and $Y$ be a right-invariant vector field on $G$. Prove that $[X,Y]=0$.

Show that the flows of $X$ and $Y$ commute.

Let $\varphi_t$ and $\psi_s$ denote the flows of $X$ and $Y$, respectively. Fix $g\in G$ and let $h=\varphi_t(g)$. By the right invariance of $Y$, $$ \psi_s(h) = \psi_s(gg^{-1}h) = \psi_s(g) g^{-1}h, $$ and by the left invariance of $X$, $$ \varphi_t(\psi_s(g)) = \varphi_t(\psi_s(g)g^{-1}g) = \psi_s(g)g^{-1} \varphi_t(g) = \psi_s(g) g^{-1}h. $$ This shows $\psi_s(\varphi_t(g))=\varphi_t(\psi_h(g))$. Since $g$ was arbitrary, we have $[X,Y]=0$.
