A particle travels a unit distance in unit time, starting and finishing at rest. Show that at some moment it must experience an acceleration or deceleration of $4$ or more.

Assume the claim is false and make use of the fundamental theorem of calculus, more than once, to reach a contradiction.

Equivalently, if $f:[0,1] \to \RR$ is twice differentiable with $f(0)=0$, $f(1)=1$, and $f'(0)=f'(1)=0$, we have to show that $|f''(x)| \geq 4$ for some $x \in (0,1)$. Assume by way of contradiction that $|f''|<4$ in $(0,1)$. Then, for every $x \in (0,1)$, $$ f'(x) = f'(x)-f'(0) = \int_0^x f''(t) \, dt < 4x $$ and similarly $$ f'(x) = f'(x)-f'(1) = - \int_x^1 f''(t) \, dt < 4(1-x). $$ If we set $g(x):=\min \{ 4x , 4(1-x) \}$, it follows that $f' < g$ in $(0,1)$. But this leads to $$ 1=f(1)-f(0)=\int_0^1 f'(t) \, dt < \int_0^1 g(t) \, dt = 1. $$
