Let $A_1, \ldots, A_n \in \M_n(\CC)$ be commuting nilpotent matrices. Show that $A_1 \cdots A_n=0$.

Consider the case where every $A_i$ is upper triangular.

The $A_i$ being commuting, there is a basis of $\CC^n$ in which they are simultaneously upper triangular. Thus, without loss of generality we may assume the $A_i$ are upper triangular to begin with. Then the entries along the main diagonal of each $A_i$ are zero because it is nilpotent. It follows that $A_1A_2$ has its $(i,j)$ entry equal to $0$ if $i \geq j-1$. Inductively, $A_1A_2 \cdots A_k$ has its $(i,j)$ entry equal to $0$ if $i \geq j-k+1$. For $k=n$ this proves $A_1A_2 \cdots A_n=0$.

Remark. The condition that the $A_i$ commute is necessary, as shown by the example $$ A_1= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \qquad A_2= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}. $$
