Let $M$ be a smooth manifold with finite fundamental group and consider a universal covering $p:{\tilde M} \to M$. Prove that for every $k$, the linear map $p^{\ast}: H^k(M) \rightarrow H^k({\tilde M})$ is injective.

Think of the idea of averaging forms on $\tilde M$ over the deck group of $p$.

Take a cohomology class $[\alpha ]\in H^k(M)$ and suppose ${\tilde \alpha}:=p^{\ast}\alpha$ is exact, so ${\tilde \alpha}=d{\tilde \beta}$ for some $k-1$ form $\tilde \beta$ on $\tilde M$. By the assumption on $\pi_1(M)$, the deck group $\Gamma$ of $p$ is finite. We have $f^{\ast}{\tilde \alpha}={\tilde \alpha}$ for every $f \in \Gamma$, so \begin{align*} {\tilde \alpha} & =\frac{1}{|\Gamma|} \sum_{f \in \Gamma} f^{\ast}{\tilde \alpha} =\frac{1}{|\Gamma|} \sum_{f \in \Gamma} f^{\ast}(d{\tilde \beta}) =\frac{1}{|\Gamma|} \sum_{f \in \Gamma} d(f^{\ast}{\tilde \beta}) \\ & = d \left( \frac{1}{|\Gamma|} \sum_{f \in \Gamma} f^{\ast}{\tilde \beta} \right) =: d{\tilde \gamma}. \end{align*} Evidently the form $\tilde \gamma$ defined above is $\Gamma$-invariant, so it descends to a $(k-1)$-form $\gamma$ on $M$ with $p^{\ast}\gamma={\tilde \gamma}$, from which it follows that $\alpha=d\gamma$.
