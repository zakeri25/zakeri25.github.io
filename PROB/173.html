Suppose $p$ is a prime and $G$ is a non-abelian group of order $p^3$. Show that the center $Z$ of $G$ coincides with the commutator subgroup $G'$.

Argue that $|Z|=p$.

$Z$ is non-trivial since $G$ is a $p$-group and is proper since $G$ is non-abelian. Moreover, $|Z| \neq p^2$ since otherwise $G/Z$ would be cyclic and $G$ would be abelian. Thus, $|Z|=p$. Now $|G/Z|=p^2$ shows that $G/Z$ is abelian and therefore $G' \subset Z$. But $G'$ is non-trivial since $G$ is non-abelian, so $G'=Z$.
