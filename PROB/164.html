Let $p(a)$ be the perimeter of a near-circle ellipse with semi-axes $a>1$ and $b=1$, where $a$ is very close to $1$. Which of the following is a better approximation for $p(a)$: $\pi(a+1)$ or $2\pi\sqrt{a}$?

Set $\ve:=a^2-1$ and expand $p(a)$ in powers of $\ve$.

The answer is $\pi(a+1)$. Set $\ve:=a^2-1$ and use the parametrization of the ellipse $x=a \cos t, y=\sin t$ for $0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$ to write \begin{align*} p(a) & =\int_0^{2\pi} \sqrt{a^2 \sin^2 t + \cos^2 t} \, dt = \int_0^{2\pi} \sqrt{1 + \ve \sin^2 t} \, dt \\ & = \int_0^{2\pi} \left( 1+ \frac{1}{2} \ve \sin^2 t - \frac{1}{8} \ve^2 \sin^4 t + O(\ve^3) \right) dt = 2\pi \left( 1 + \frac{1}{4} \ve - \frac{3}{64} \ve^2 + O(\ve^3) \right). \end{align*} Now $$ \pi(a+1)= \pi (1+(1+\ve)^{1/2}) = 2\pi \left( 1+\frac{1}{4} \ve - \frac{1}{16} \ve^2 + O(\ve^3) \right) $$ while $$ 2\pi \sqrt{a} = 2 \pi (1+\ve)^{1/4} = 2\pi \left( 1+\frac{1}{4} \ve - \frac{3}{32} \ve^2 + O(\ve^3) \right). $$ So the error for $\pi(a+1)$ is roughly $2\pi (\ve^2/64)$ while the error for $2\pi \sqrt{a}$ is roughly $2\pi (3\ve^2/64)$, i.e., three times as much.
