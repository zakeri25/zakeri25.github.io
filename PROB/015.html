Let $\omega$ be a left-invariant $n$-form on an $n$-dimensional compact connected Lie group $G$. Prove that $\omega$ is also right-invariant.

If $R_g$ is the right multiplication by $g$, first show that $R_g^{\ast} \, \omega = f(g) \, \omega$, where $f: G \to \RR \sm \{ 0 \}$ is smooth.

We may assume $\omega$ is non-vanishing. Let $R_g$ and $L_g$ denote the right and left multiplication by $g \in G$, respectively. Consider $R_g^{\ast} \, \omega$. This $n$-form is left-invariant since \begin{align*} L_h^{\ast} (R_g^{\ast} \, \omega) & = (R_g\circ L_h)^{\ast} \, \omega = (L_h\circ R_g)^{\ast} \, \omega \\ & = R_g^{\ast} (L_h^{\ast} \, \omega) = R_g^{\ast} \, \omega. \end{align*} Now at each $h\in G$, $(R_g^{\ast} \omega)(h)=f(g,h) \, \omega(h)$, where $f$ is a smooth real-valued function. Since both $\omega$ and $R_g^{\ast} \, \omega$ are left-invariant, $f$ does not depend on $h$. So $f=f(g)\neq 0$. Since $R_{gg'}=R_{g'}\circ R_g$, we have $f(gg')=f(g)f(g')$, so $f:G\rightarrow \RR \sm \{ 0 \}$ is a group homomorphism. It follows that $f(G)$ is a connected compact subgroup of $\RR \sm \{ 0 \}$. Thus $f=1$ everywhere and $R_g^{\ast} \, \omega=\omega$.

Remark. The compactness condition is necessary. To see this, let $G$ be the upper half-plane $\{ x+iy \in \CC : y>0 \}$ equipped with the structure of a Lie group via identification with the affine group $\{ t \mapsto y t+x \}$. Then the hyperbolic area form $\omega= y^{-2} dx \wedge dy$ is left-invariant but not right-invariant.
