Let $A_1, \ldots, A_n$ be subsets of a finite set $X$. If the $A_i$ are pairwise disjoint, then clearly $\sum_{i=1}^n |A_i| \leq |X|$. Prove the following generalization: Suppose for each $1 \leq i \leq n$ there is an integer $1 \leq k_i \leq n$ such that every element of $A_i$ lies in at most $k_i$ sets among $A_1, \ldots, A_n$. Then $\sum_{i=1}^n |A_i|/k_i \leq |X|$.

Write the sum as a double sum and switch the order.

For each $x \in X$, let $I(x)=\{ i: x \in A_i \}$. By the assumption, $|I(x)| \leq k_i$ if $i \in I(x)$ and therefore $|I(x)| \leq m(x):=\min_{i \in I(x)} k_i$. Now \begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{|A_i|}{k_i} & = \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{x \in A_i} \frac{1}{k_i} \\ & = \sum_{x \in X} \sum_{i \in I(x)} \frac{1}{k_i} \leq \sum_{x \in X} \sum_{i \in I(x)} \frac{1}{m(x)} \\ & = \sum_{x \in X} \frac{|I(x)|}{m(x)} \leq \sum_{x \in X} 1 = |X|. \end{align*} Remark. The statement easily extends to the case of a finite measure space $(X,\mu)$ and measurable subsets $A_1, \ldots, A_n$. Simply define $I(x)$ and $m(x)$ as above and note that \begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{\mu(A_i)}{k_i} & = \sum_{i=1}^n \int_X \frac{\chi_{A_i}(x)}{k_i} d\mu(x) \\ & = \int_X \left(\sum_{i=1}^n \frac{\chi_{A_i}(x)}{k_i} \right) d\mu(x) = \int_X \left(\sum_{i\in I(x)} \frac{1}{k_i} \right) d\mu(x) \\ & \leq \int_X \left( \sum_{i \in I(x)} \frac{1}{m(x)} \right) d\mu(x) = \int_X \frac{|I(x)|}{m(x)} d\mu(x) \leq \int_X d\mu(x)= \mu(X). \end{align*}
