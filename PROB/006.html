Let $V$ be an infinite-dimensional vector space over some field. For subspaces $A$ and $B$ of $V$, write $A \thicksim B$ if $(A+B)/(A\cap B)$ is finite-dimensional. Prove that $\thicksim$ is an equivalence relation.

To show transitivity, use the following two facts on subspaces of $V$: \begin{align*} X/(X \cap Y) & \cong (X+Y)/Y \\ X/Y & \cong (X/Z) / (Y/Z) \quad \text{if} \quad Z \subset Y \subset X. \end{align*}

Only transitivity is non-trivial. Let us write f.d. for finite-dimensional. Suppose $A \thicksim B$ and $B \thicksim C$. Then $$ (A\cap B)/(A\cap B\cap C) \cong ((A\cap B)+C)/C \subset (B+C)/C $$ is f.d., and similarly $(B\cap C)/(A\cap B\cap C)$ is f.d. Therefore, $A/(A\cap B\cap C)$ is f.d. since its quotient by the f.d. subspace $(A\cap B)/(A\cap B\cap C)$ is the f.d. space $A/(A \cap B)$. Similarly, $C/(A\cap B\cap C)$ is f.d. It follows that $(A+C)/(A\cap B\cap C)$ is f.d. and so its quotient $(A+C)/(A\cap C)$ is f.d. Hence $A\thicksim C$.
