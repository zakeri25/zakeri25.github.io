If $A_{3\times 2}$ and $B_{2\times 3}$ are real matrices with $$ AB= \begin{bmatrix*}[rrr] 8 & 2 & -2\\ 2 & 5 & 4 \\ -2 & 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix*}, $$ prove that $BA=9\ I_{2\times 2}$.

First check that $(AB)^2=9(AB)$.

A brief computation shows that $(AB)^2=9(AB)$. Also, $AB$ has nullity $1$, which shows $B$ has nullity $1$. In particular, $B$ is surjective and $A$ is injective. Pick any $y \in \RR^2$ and write $y=B(x)$ for some $x\in \RR^3$. Then $ABAB(x)-9AB(x)=0$ so $BAB(x)-9B(x)=0$ so $BA(y)-9y=0$. Since $y$ was arbitrary, $BA=9I$.
