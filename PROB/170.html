Show that every simple group of order $60$ is isomorphic to the alternating group $A_5$.

Show that such a group has $6$ distinct $5$-Sylow subgroups, and use it to embed the group into $A_6$ first.

Let $G$ be a simple group of order $60$. The number $n_5$ of $5$-Sylow subgroups of $G$ satisfies $n_5 \equiv 1$ (mod $5$) and divides $60/5=12$, so $n_5=6$. The action of $G$ by conjugation on these $5$-Sylow subgroups defines a homomorphism $G \to S_6$ with trivial kernel. The composition $G \to S_6 \to \ZZ_2$ with the signature homomorphism must be trivial, otherwise $G$ would have an index $2$ subgroup contrary to its simplicity. Thus, we may assume without loss of generality that $G$ is a simple subgroup of $A_6$. Now the action of $G$ on the six left cosets of $G$ in $A_6$ defines a homomorphism $\phi: G \to S_6$ with trivial kernel, hence $\phi(G) \cong G$. Moreover, elements of $\phi(G)$ share a fixed point (the coset $eG$) so they must all be even permutations of the remaining five elements. This shows $\phi(G) \cong A_5$.
