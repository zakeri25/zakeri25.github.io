Prove that the $2$-sphere cannot be made into a topological group.

Left multiplication in a topological group has no fixed points.

Assume by way of contradiction that $\Sen^2$ can be equipped with the structure of a topological group. The left multiplication $L: \Sen^2 \to \Sen^2$ defined by $L(x)=gx$ for some $g\neq 1$ is a homeomorphism with no fixed points. Hence, by the Lefschetz fixed point formula, $$ \sum_{j=0}^2 (-1)^j \ \tr(L_{\ast}:H_j(\Sen^2) \to H_j(\Sen^2))=0. $$ But $L$ is homotopic to the identity map of $\Sen^2$, so the above sum must be equal to the Euler characteristic of the sphere, namely $2$.

Remark. The proof shows that the Euler characteristic of any compact topological group must be zero. It is known that $\Sen^n$ admits the structure of a topological group only when $n=0,1,3$.
