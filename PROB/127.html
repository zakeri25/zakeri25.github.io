Show that for every sequence $\{ x_n \}$ in the interval $[0,1]$ the series $f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \dfrac{1}{n^2|x_n-x|} $ converges a.e. on $[0,1]$.

Consider the set of points in $[0,1]$ at distance $\geq \ve/n^2$ to $x_n$ for every $n$, and show that $f$ is integrable on this set.

Let $B(a,r):= \{ x: |x-a| < r \}$. For any $a \in [0,1]$, the inequality $$ \int_{[0,1]\sm B(a,r)} \frac{dx}{|x-a|} \leq C \log \Big( \frac{1}{r} \Big) $$ holds, where $C > 0$ is a universal constant. Take a decreasing sequence $\ve_k \to 0$ and define $$ E_k:=[0,1] \sm \bigcup_n B \Big( x_n, \frac{\ve_k}{n^2} \Big). $$ The $E_k$ form an increasing sequence of closed sets with $|E_k| \geq 1- 2\ve_k \sum_n 1/n^2$, so $E:= \bigcup_k E_k$ is a full-measure set in $[0,1]$. Now for each $k$, \begin{align*} \int_{E_k} f(x)\, dx & = \sum_n \frac{1}{n^2} \int_{E_k} \frac{dx}{|x-x_n|} \leq \sum_n \frac{1}{n^2} \int_{[0,1]\sm B(x_n,\ve_k/n^2)} \frac{dx}{|x-x_n|} \\ & \leq C \sum_n \frac{2 \log n - \log \ve_k}{n^2} <+\infty, \end{align*} which shows $f(x) < +\infty$ a.e. on $E_k$. It follows that $f(x) < +\infty$ a.e. on $E$.
