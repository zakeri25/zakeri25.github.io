Let $U,U'\subset \DD$ be open topological disks and $f:U \to U'$ be a conformal map which preserves the hyperbolic metric of $\DD$. Prove that $f$ extends to a conformal automorphism $\DD \to \DD$.

Reduce to the case when $U,U'$ contain the origin and $f(0)=0$.

By pre- and post-composing $f$ with appropriate disk automorphisms we may assume that $U$ and $U'$ contain the origin and $f(0)=0$. The isometry condition then shows that there is a small $r_0 > 0$ such that $|f(z)|=|z|$ whenever $|z| \leq r_0$. By the open mapping theorem $f$ restricts to a rigid rotation $z \mapsto \lambda z$ of the disk $|z| \leq r_0$. Hence it extends to the disk automorphism $z \mapsto \lambda z$ by analytic continuation.

Remark. Here is an alternative argument. Assuming as before that $f(0)=0$, the isometry condition implies $|f'(0)|=1$. By further composing $f$ with a rotation, we may arrange $f'(0)=1$. If $f\neq \id$, by the theorem of Leau-Fatou, there are "attracting directions" for $f$ near the origin along which the orbit of a point under the iterations of $f$ tends to $0$. Evidently such $f$ cannot be an isometry in any neighborhood of the origin.
