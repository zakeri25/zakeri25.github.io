Suppose $f$ is holomorphic in a neighborhood of the closed unit disk and satisfies $\myre(\bar{z}f(z))>0$ whenever $|z|=1$. Show that $f$ has a unique zero in $\DD$.

Apply Rouché's theorem to a pair of suitable functions.

The hypothesis $\myre(\bar{z}f(z))>0$ implies $|\bar{z}f(z)-1|<|\bar{z}f(z)+1|$ or $|f(z)-z|<|f(z)+z|$ whenever $|z|=1$. This inequality can be written as $|(f(z)+z)-2z|<|f(z)+z|$, so by Rouché's theorem, $f(z)+z$ and $2z$ have the same number of zeros in $\DD$. It can also be written $|(f(z)+z)-2f(z)|<|f(z)+z|$, which by Rouché shows $f(z)+z$ and $2f(z)$ have the same number of zeros in $\DD$. Thus, $f(z)$ and $z$ have the same number of zeros in $\DD$.

Alternative solution. Let $c=\min_{|z|=1} \myre(\bar{z}f(z))>0$. Then $0 \leq \myre(\bar{z}f(z)-c) < \myre(\bar{z}f(z))$ and $\myim(\bar{z}f(z)-c) = \myim(\bar{z}f(z))$ whenever $|z|=1$, hence \begin{align*} |\bar{z}f(z)-c|^2 & = (\myre(\bar{z}f(z)-c))^2+(\myim(\bar{z}f(z)-c))^2 \\ & < (\myre(\bar{z}f(z)))^2+(\myim(\bar{z}f(z)))^2=|\bar{z}f(z)|^2. \end{align*} This shows $|f(z)-cz|<|f(z)|$ whenever $|z|=1$, which by Rouché's theorem implies that $f(z)$ and $cz$ have the same number of zeros in $\DD$.
