Let $f(x)=2x(1-x)$. Compute $\ds{\int_0^1 f^{\circ n}(x) \, dx}$, where $f^{\circ n}$ is the $n$-th iterate of $f$ for $n \geq 1$.

$f$ is affinely conjugate to a simple quadratic polynomial.

The quadratic polynomial $f$ has a fixed critical point at $1/2$ so it must be affinely conjugate to $g(x)=x^2$. In fact, $f=\varphi^{-1} \circ g \circ \varphi$, where $\varphi(x)=-2x+1$. Hence, \begin{align*} \int_0^1 f^{\circ n}(x) \, dx & = \int_0^1 (\varphi^{-1} \circ g^{\circ n} \circ \varphi)(x) \, dx \\ & = -\frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 ((-2x+1)^{2^n}-1) \, dx = \frac{2^{n-1}}{2^n+1} . \end{align*}
