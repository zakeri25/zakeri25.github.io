Let $\Sigma \subset \RR^m$ be a compact smooth $(m-1)$-dimensional submanifold. Show that $\int_\Sigma \bn \ dS=0$, where $\bn:\Sigma \rightarrow \RR^m$ is the outward unit normal to $\Sigma$ and $dS$ denotes the induced Riemannian volume on $\Sigma$.

Use the divergence theorem.

Let $x_1, \ldots, x_m$ be the standard coordinates of $\RR^m$ and $\bn=\sum_{j=1}^m n_j \, \frac{\bd}{\bd x_j}$. It is enough to check that $\int_\Sigma n_j\, dS=0$ for all $j$. But this follows from the divergence theorem since $$ \int_\Sigma n_j\, dS = \int_\Sigma \frac{\bd}{\bd x_j} \cdot \bn \, dS = \int_{\text{int}(\Sigma)} \operatorname{div} \Big( \frac{\bd}{\bd x_j} \Big) \ dx_1 \cdots dx_m=0. $$
Remark. An piecewise linear version of the above statement is the following: Let $\Sigma\subset \RR^m$ be a compact polyhedron with $(m-1)$-dimensional faces $S_1,S_2, \cdots , S_k$. Let $\bn$ be the outward unit normal defined in the union of the interiors of the $S_j$ (so the restriction of $\bn$ to the interior of each $S_j$ is a constant vector field). Then $\sum_{j=1}^k \bn|_{S_j} \operatorname{vol}_{m-1}(S_j)=0$.
