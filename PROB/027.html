Prove that every finitely-generated and finitely-presented group can be obtained as the fundamental group of a compact $4$-dimensional manifold.

Think of a connected sum $X$ of finitely many copies of $\Sen^1 \times \Sen^3$ and show that any relator in the fundamental group of $X$ can be killed by a suitable surgery on a tubular neighborhood of the representing loop.

Let $G$ be a finitely-generated and finitely-presented group. Then $G=\langle F_n | R_1, \ldots, R_s \rangle$, where $F_n$ is the free group on $n$ generators and $R_1, \ldots, R_s$ are finitely many relators. Set $X:=\#_{j=1}^n (\Sen^1 \times \Sen^3)$. Since $\pi_1(\Sen^1 \times \Sen^3)=\ZZ$, we have $\pi_1(X)=F_n$. Take any homotopy class $[\gamma_j] \in \pi_1(X)$ which represents $R_j$. By putting $\gamma_j$ in general position, we may assume that it is homeomorphic to a circle. Then the tubular neighborhood of $\gamma_j$ in $X$ is homeomorphic to $\Sen^1\times D^3$ whose boundary is $\Sen^1 \times \Sen^2$ (here $D^k$ denotes the closed $k$-dimensional ball, with $\bd D^k = \SS^{k-1}$). This is also the boundary of $D^2 \times \Sen^2$, so we can remove this tubular neighborhood and glue back a copy of $D^2 \times \Sen^2$ in which $\gamma_j$ is homotopically trivial. Applying this surgery for $1\leq j\leq s$, we obtain the desired $4$-manifold.
