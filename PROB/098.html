Suppose $H,K$ are subgroups of a group $G$ such that $[G:H]=[G:K]=n$ and $[G:H \cap K]=n(n-1)$ for some $n \geq 2$. Show that $H$ and $K$ are conjugate subgroups.

The set $HK$ is a union of left cosets of $K$ and right cosets of $H$. How many of each?

The set $HK$ is a disjoint union $\bigcup_i h_i K$ of left cosets of $K$ and a disjoint union $\bigcup_i H k_i$ of right cosets of $H$. We have $[HK:H]=[K:H \cap K]=n-1$ and similarly $[HK:K]=[H:H \cap K]=n-1$, so both decompositions have $n-1$ cosets. It follows that $G \sm HK$ is a single left coset of $K$ and at the same time a single right coset of $H$. Thus, $gK=Hg$ for some $g \in G$, which proves $gKg^{-1}=H$.

Remark. Here is an alternative solution assuming $G$ is finite. Let $|H \cap K|=m$, so $|H|=|K|=m(n-1)$ and $|G|=mn(n-1)$. Decompose $G$ into the disjoint union $\bigcup_g HgK$ of double cosets of $H$ and $K$, where $$ |HgK|=|HgKg^{-1}|=\frac{|H||K|}{|H \cap gKg^{-1}|}. $$ For the double coset $HK$, this size is $m(n-1)^2$ while for other double cosets this size is at least $m(n-1)$. Since $m(n-1)^2+m(n-1)=mn(n-1)$, we conclude that $G$ is the union of precisely two double cosets $HK$ and $HgK$, with $|H \cap gKg^{-1}|=m(n-1)=|H|$. This gives $H \subset gKg^{-1}$ and since the two subgroups have the same order we conclude that $H=gKg^{-1}$.
