Find all pairs of complex numbers $z,w$ with $|z|=|w|=1$ such that $z+w+z/w$ is real.

Work with the representation $z=e^{it}, w=e^{is}$.

Set $z=e^{it}, w=e^{is}$ for real $t,s$. Then $z+w+z/w$ is real iff \begin{align*} \sin t + \sin s + \sin (t-s)=0 & \Longleftrightarrow \sin t + \sin s + \sin t \cos s - \cos t \sin s=0 \\ & \Longleftrightarrow \sin t (1+\cos s) + \sin s (1-\cos t) =0 \\ & \Longleftrightarrow \sin(t/2) \cos(t/2) \cos^2(s/2) + \sin(s/2) \cos(s/2) \sin^2(t/2) =0 \\ & \Longleftrightarrow \sin(t/2) \cos(s/2) \big( \cos(t/2)\cos(s/2) + \sin(s/2)\sin(t/2) \big) =0 \\ & \Longleftrightarrow \sin(t/2) \cos(s/2) \cos((t-s)/2)=0 \end{align*} This gives the solutions $t=0, s=\pi, t-s=\pi$ (mod $2\pi$). Thus, the possibilities for $(z,w)$ are $(1,w)$, $(z,-1)$, and $(z,-z)$ where $|z|=1$ and $|w|=1$.
