Suppose $f(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n$ is holomorphic in the unit disk $\DD$. If $|a_k| = r^{-k} \sup_{|z|=r} |f(z)|$ for some $k \geq 0$ and $0 < r < 1$, show that $f(z)=a_k z^k$.

Parseval's formula would give a quick proof.

Let $M(r):=\sup_{|z|=r}|f(z)|$. By Parseval's formula, $$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} |a_n|^2 r^{2n} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} |f(re^{it})|^2 \, dt. $$ Hence, $$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} |a_n|^2 r^{2n} \leq (M(r))^2 = |a_k|^2 r^{2k} \Longrightarrow a_n=0 \quad \text{for all} \ n \neq k. $$
Remark. The classical Cauchy's estimates in complex analysis show that $|a_n| \leq r^{-n} M(r)$ for all $n \geq 0$ and $0 < r < 1$. The above statement shows that equality occurs for some $n$ and $r$ iff $f(z)$ is a pure power of $z$. Notice the remarkable strength of Parseval's formula compared to Cauchy's estimates: While the latter gives the $\ell^{\infty}$ bound $\sup_n |a_n|r^n \leq M(r)$, the former gives the much sharper $\ell^2$ bound $(\sum_n |a_n|^2 r^{2n})^{1/2} \leq M(r)$.
