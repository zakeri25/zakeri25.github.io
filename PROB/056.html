Suppose $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ are $n>3$ real numbers such that $\sum_{j=1}^n a_j \geq n$ and $\sum_{j=1}^n a_j^2 \geq n^2$. Show that $\max \{ a_1 , \ldots, a_n \} \geq 2$.

*** NEED A GOOD ELEMENTARY PROOF***

Set $a = \max \{ a_1 , \ldots, a_n \} \geq 1$. Consider the non-empty compact convex set $K \subset \RR^n$ defined by the $n+1$ inequalities $x_1 \leq a, \ldots, x_n \leq a, \sum x_j \geq n$. The square-distance function $(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \mapsto \sum x_j^2$ takes its maximum value on $K$ at an extreme point $(p_1, \ldots, p_n) \in \bd K$. There are two possibilities:

1. $p_j=a$ for all $j$. In this case $$ n^2 \leq \sum a_j^2 \leq \sum p_j^2 = na^2. $$ This gives $a^2 \geq n \geq 4$, or $a \geq 2$.

2. There is a $1 \leq k \leq n$ such that $p_j=a$ for all $j \neq k$ and $p_k = n-(n-1)a$. In this case $$ n^2 \leq \sum a_j^2 \leq \sum p_j^2 = (n-1)a^2+(n-(n-1)a)^2. $$ This gives $n(n-1)a^2-2n(n-1)a \geq 0$, or $a \geq 2$, as required.
