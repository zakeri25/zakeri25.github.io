Let $H,K$ be subgroups of a group $G$ with finite indices $n=[G:H]$ and $m=[G,K]$ such that $\gcd(n,m)=1$. Prove that $G=HK$.

Consider the natural map $H/(H \cap K) \to G/K$ between coset spaces.

The coset space map $H/(H \cap K) \to G/K$ given by $h(H \cap K) \mapsto hK$ is well defined and injective, so $a:=[H: H \cap K]$ is finite and $a \leq m$. Similarly, $b:=[K:H \cap K]$ is finite and $b \leq n$. Now $[G:H \cap K]=na=mb$ is a common multiple of $n,m$ so it must be a multiple of $nm$ since $\gcd(n,m)=1$. This gives $nm \leq na$ and $nm \leq mb$, so $a=m, b=n$. It follows that the coset map above is bijective. In particular, for any $g \in G$ there is an $h \in H$ such that $gK = hK$. This gives $h^{-1}g \in K$, or $g \in HK$.
