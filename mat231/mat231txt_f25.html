MATH 231 Text and References

The main text used for our course is
    D. Poole, Linear Algebra, A Modern Introduction, 4th edition, Cengage Learning, 2014.
The most cost-effective option for many of you is a single-term subscription to WebAssign which also gives you access to the electronic version of the book. Check out the following link:

Textbook + WebAssign

As a Queens College student you are eligible for a discounted price of $87.

My lectures and Poole's textbook provide enough material for you during this semester. Here are additional optional references that can be used as guides, supplementary readings, or sources of examples and problems:
    D. Margalit and J.Rabinoff, Interactive Linear Algebra, (free online textbook, has clear explanations and nice visual and interactive examples).
    J. Hefferon, Linear Algebra, 4rd edition, (free online textbook, includes many problems and solutions).
The following books require more mathematical maturity and are highly recommended if you are interested in theoretical aspects and plan to major in pure mathematics:
    K. Hoffman and R. Kunze, Linear Algebra, 2nd edition, Prentice Hall, 1971.
    S. Axler, Linear Algebra done right, 3rd edition, Springer-Verlag, 2015.
    P. Halmos, Finite dimensional vector spaces, Springer-Verlag, 1974.
