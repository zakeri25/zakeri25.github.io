Prove that every connected one-dimensional smooth manifold $M$ is diffeomorphic to the line or to the circle.

Construct a non-zero smooth vector field on the universal covering of $M$ and look at its flow.

Consider the universal covering $\tilde M$. Equip $\tilde{M}$ with a smooth Riemannian metric and use it to construct a non-vanishing smooth $1$-form on $\tilde M$. The dual of this $1$-from is a non-vanishing smooth vector field on $\tilde M$. Consider the trajectory $t \mapsto \varphi_p(t)$ of this vector field with the initial condition $p \in \tilde M$. The maximal extension of $\varphi_p$ over some interval $]a,b[\subset \RR$ exhibits a diffeomorphism between $]a,b[$ and $\tilde M$: it is one-to-one because $\tilde{M}$ is simply connected and it is onto because each trajectory is open in $\tilde M$. So $\tilde M$ is diffeomorphic to $\RR$. Now the group of covering transformations of $\tilde{M}\rightarrow M$ is either trivial or isomorphic to $\ZZ$. Hence $M$ is diffeomorphic either to the line or to the circle.
